How Joule works A single number, in joules, for every choice you make.

Joule converts your trip into a single score from 0 to 100. A higher score means your choice puts less pressure on the state's fuel reserve — and the transport network has more room to absorb it.

60%Fuel impact + 25%Corridor load + 15%Time cost to you

Your fuel impact is measured in kilowatt-hour-equivalents — so electricity, petrol, and diesel all sit on one axis. Corridor load reads live bus and train capacity on the line you're using: if your train has spare seats, your walk-on trip is worth more to the state. Time cost keeps the score honest — if the alternative takes two hours longer, Joule won't pretend that's a free swap.

Joule never tells you not to travel. The goal is the opposite: keep New South Wales moving, with one fewer barrel of imported fuel behind every journey.

Joule

A public-facing energy accounting service concept for Transport for NSW and NSW Climate & Energy Action. Joule is an awareness tool — it does not collect, track, or log your trips.
